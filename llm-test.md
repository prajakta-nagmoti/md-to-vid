# Large Language Models (LLMs)

Large Language Models are neural networks trained on massive text corpora to predict the next token in a sequence.

## How They Work

- Input text is split into tokens.
- A transformer model processes token relationships using attention.
- The model predicts likely next tokens repeatedly to form responses.

## Why They Matter

- They can summarize, explain, translate, and generate code.
- They improve productivity in writing and software workflows.

## Key Limits

- They can hallucinate incorrect facts.
- Output quality depends on prompt clarity and context.
- They require validation for critical tasks.

## Safe Use Tips

- Ask for sources or reasoning steps.
- Verify important claims independently.
- Keep humans in the loop for final decisions.
